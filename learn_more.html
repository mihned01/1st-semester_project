Understanding Plastic Pollution and Global Solutions

Explore the global efforts and essential facts that are shaping the fight against plastic pollution.

The Lifecycle of Plastic

Explore how plastic moves through its lifecycle and impacts our planet.

Production

Plastic is made from fossil fuels, primarily oil and natural gas, through an energy-intensive process.

Usage

Plastic is used in countless products, from packaging to household items, often for single-use purposes.

Disposal

After use, plastic is disposed of in landfills, incinerated, or improperly discarded, leading to pollution.

Pollution

Improperly disposed plastic pollutes our oceans and land, taking centuries to decompose.

Shocking Truths About Plastic

Over 8 million tons of plastic enter the oceans every year, threatening marine life.

Plastic takes up to 1,000 years to break down in the environment.

Plastic bags take up to 1,000 years to break down and are among the most common items found in litter surveys.

The Great Pacific Garbage Patch is a floating mass of plastic waste covering an area roughly twice the size of Texas.

Plastic pollution is found in the Arctic, with even remote areas affected by waste carried by ocean currents.

Plastic waste in the ocean kills over 1 million seabirds and 100,000 marine mammals annually.

Around 79% of plastic waste ends up in landfills or the environment, rather than being recycled.

Microplastics have been found in human drinking water, air, and even in food products.

Over 60% of all plastic waste is used for packaging, which is typically used only once before being discarded.

More than 400 million tons of plastic are produced globally each year.

Nearly 90% of sea turtles have ingested plastic, mistaking it for food.

Less than 10% of plastic used globally is recycled into new products.

The production of plastic releases large amounts of greenhouse gases, contributing to climate change.

Recycling one ton of plastic can save up to 5,774 kWh of energy, enough to power a home for 6 months.

By 2050, it's estimated there will be more plastic than fish in the oceans (by weight).

The Global Plastic Treaty

What is the Global Plastic Treaty?

The Global Plastic Treaty is an international agreement aimed at addressing the growing plastic pollution crisis. By fostering global collaboration, the treaty aims to mitigate the environmental, health, and economic impacts of plastic pollution.

Key Objectives of the Global Plastic Treaty

The treaty focuses on reducing plastic production, improving recycling systems, and minimizing plastic waste. It urges countries to implement policies that reduce plastic usage, encourage better waste management practices, and advance circular economy models.

Global Impact and Collaboration

With support from many countries, the Global Plastic Treaty is a step toward global cooperation in solving the plastic pollution crisis. It encourages nations to collaborate on innovative solutions, share technologies, and set national targets for plastic waste reduction.
